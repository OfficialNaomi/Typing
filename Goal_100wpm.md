# Typing Speed Improvement Plan: The Road to 100 WPM

## The Core Philosophy: Accuracy over Raw Speed
At 70 WPM, basic muscle memory is established, but fine motor errors and hesitation are the primary bottlenecks. Hitting backspace destroys rhythm and flow. 
**Goal:** Reach a baseline of 100 WPM with strictly >98% accuracy.

## Phase 1: Eradicating Weak Keys (Weeks 1-2)
The focus here is entirely on retraining muscle memory for specific problem keys without the cognitive load of reading actual sentences.

*   **Tool:** [Keybr.com](https://www.keybr.com/) or Linux native `gtypist`.
*   **Method:** Allow the algorithm to identify the weakest keys (often pinky or ring finger reaches).
*   **Rule:** Do not move to the next key subset until the current set is consistently typed with 98%+ accuracy. Ignore the overall WPM metric entirely during this phase.

## Phase 2: N-gram & Bigram Mastery (Weeks 3-4)
Fast typists don't type letter-by-letter; they type in common combinations (e.g., "th", "he", "ing", "tion"). 

*   **Tool:** [Monkeytype](https://monkeytype.com/) (custom practice settings for n-grams) or [RanelPad](https://ranelpad.com/).
*   **Method:** Drill specific high-frequency English bigrams and trigrams until they become a single fluid motion.
*   **Rule:** Slow down immediately if accuracy drops below 96%. 

## Phase 3: Contextual Flow & Punctuation (Weeks 5-6)
Moving from 80 WPM to 100+ requires looking ahead in the text and maintaining flow through capitalization and punctuation.

*   **Tool:** [Monkeytype](https://monkeytype.com/) (Quotes mode, English 10k vocabulary, punctuation and numbers ON).
*   **Method:** Keep your eyes one or two words ahead of what your fingers are currently typing.
*   **Rule:** The `Ctrl + Backspace` rule. If you make a mistake, stop, delete the *entire* word using `Ctrl + Backspace` (or `Alt + Backspace`), and retype it. Never delete single letters—this breaks the mental map of the whole word.

## Daily Routine Strategy
Consistency over duration. A 25-minute focused session is better than a 2-hour marathon.
1.  **Warm-up (5 mins):** Keybr or `gtypist` (pure accuracy, deliberately slow).
2.  **Targeted Drill (10 mins):** Specific weak fingers or n-gram repetition.
3.  **Application (10 mins):** Monkeytype Quotes mode to practice flow and reading ahead.

## The "100 WPM" Milestones
- [ ] Achieve 98% consistent accuracy on Keybr across the entire alphabet.
- [ ] Consistently hit 90+ WPM on 60-second standard tests.
- [ ] Hit 100 WPM peak on short bursts (15-word tests).
- [ ] **Final Goal:** Reach 100 WPM on a full 60-second test with punctuation.
